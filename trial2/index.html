Painting-Maps and Maps in Print


How the medium shapes imaginings of frontier


In 1603, Yang Shining submitted to the Wanli Emperor three-volumes of hand-drawn atlas, currently held in the Imperial Household Agency in Japan. This "Map and Essay of Defense Command Xuanfu, Datong and Three Passes of Shanxi" ((Xuanda Shanxi) Sanzhen tushuo (宣大山西)三鎮圖說) depicts military fortifications established along Sino-Mongol frontier, including the well-known Great Wall. The Map also entails fascinating details of images of Mongol tribes living beyond the Wall. This map was soon published in woodblock print and circulated outside the court among literati readers.

In this project, I compare between hand-drawn and printed map in order to analyze how the choice of medium affected the visualization of frontier spaces and foreign people.
