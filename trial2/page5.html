Timeline of Ming-Mongol Relations

1449

Tumu Debacle

Oirat Mongol army led by Esen defeated Ming army at Tumu fortress and captured Ming Yingzhong Emperor (Emperor Zhengtong) as a hostage. After this period, Ming focused on defense in northern frontiers against Mongols.

Heightened Military Crisis

The Altan Khan raided north China plain when Ming Jiajing Emperor refused his demands for increased trade. In 1550, Altan's army besieged the Ming capital of Beijing.

1550

1570

Peace Treaty

Jiajing's successor, Longqing emperor, signed the peace treaty with the Altan Khan and instituted horse markets along the border in exchange of silk.

Yang Shining made a map

Although Mongol threats were alleviated, a lingering sense of military threats continued to promote military map production. As Supreme Commander of Xuanfu, Datong, and Shanxi, Yang Shining (1537-1609) ordered his inferiors to submit maps and essays, and compiled the book titled Sanzhen tushuo.
